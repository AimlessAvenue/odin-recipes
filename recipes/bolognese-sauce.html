Ragù (Bolognese sauce)

A brief introduction:

Ragù (Ragu alla bolognese) is a rich and tasty condiment based on minced meat and tomato sauce, ideal for Pasta, Tagliatelle, Lasagne, and in general for making tasty Italian first courses (or accompanying Polenta!). A great classic of Italian cuisine, especially Bolognese cuisine, where it is a real institution! It has become world famous as a traditional specialty!

How to make an excellent Bolognese sauce at home? Follow this Ragù Recipe and all the step-by-step tips, ranging from the choice of ingredients to the various cooking stages! And you will see that you will get a meat ragu. Dense and full-bodied, creamy to perfection, tasty and very fragrant, just like the original Bolognese one!

Like every classic recipe there are different versions: the Neapolitan one with the addition of whole pieces of meat, the fish one, vegetarian versions.. and there are many variations! For example: with fried bacon, with added sausage, the exquisite one without tomato ("In bianco" or "in white" as we say in Italy!)

Today we will make the original Bolognese Ragù Recipe; precisely the one deposited by the Italian Academy of Cuisine on October 17, 1982 at the Bologna Chamber of Commerce. It is a simple preparation you just need to have some time available to dedicate to the slow cooking.

The secret lies precisely in respecting the various phases, in particular, waiting for a slow cooking! The word Ragu derives from the French “ragoûter” which means to stew over low heat! So, don't rush! Excellent minced meat that is not too lean (both beef and pork) and a full-bodied and quality tomato puree will do the rest!

The Ragù is essential in the Italian Cuisine for making the classic Lasagne alla bolognese or Tagliatelle alla bolognese, to dress fresh or dry pasta; stuff cannelloni, crepes, orecchiette, gnocchi, make tasty baked pasta and first courses perfect for the whole family! Kids love it too! in short, it's inevitable to have its in your basic recipe book! Go through it, it can be stored and frozen to have it ready for use!
